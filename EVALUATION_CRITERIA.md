# Evaluation Criteria for Design Quality Assessment

## 1. Usability
- **Ease of Use:** Can users easily understand how to use it?
- **Learnability:** How easy is it for users to accomplish basic tasks the first time they encounter the design?

## 2. Aesthetics
- **Visual Appeal:** Does the design meet the aesthetic expectations of users?
- **Consistency:** Is the style consistent across all components?

## 3. Functionality
- **Performance:** Does the design perform effectively under varying conditions?
- **Feature Completeness:** Does the design provide all the features that a user would expect?

## 4. Accessibility
- **Inclusivity:** Is the design accessible to users with different abilities?
- **Adaptability:** Can the design adjust to different devices and contexts?

## 5. Feedback
- **User Feedback:** Are mechanisms in place to get user feedback on design?
- **Iterative Improvement:** Is there a process for continuously improving the design based on feedback?  

## Conclusion
Regularly assessing these criteria helps ensure that the design remains effective and user-friendly over time.
